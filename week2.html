This second week of PHP/MySQL was an exciting adventure into what happens after you submit a HTML form. Previously, the story of HTML forms basically ended here after clicking the "submit" button:

Subscribe to Our Email List

Enter your first name:

Enter your email address:

Select your interests:

Eggplant
Peppers
Potatoes
Tomatoes

Now, with PHP, the story is just starting with clicking of the Subscribe button. PHP takes the information provided in this form and then processes the data. This week we delved into the how the action and method attribute of the HTML form sends the data in the form to the server, and how PHP works with that data. For example, this code:

<form action="process_form.php" method="post">

tells us that the form data is sent to "process_form.php" to process the data and that the "post" method is used rather than "get".

When the data is sent to "process_form.php", the information entered by the user is then accessible to the server through the $_POST['firstname'] and $_POST['email'] variables.These values can then be used in various ways, such outputting it to the user:

$firstName = $_POST['firstname']; $email = $_POST['email']; echo "Thank you for subscribing to our Email Subscriber List, $firstName
\n"; echo "We will send emails to $email
\n";
